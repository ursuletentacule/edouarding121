Introduction

Royal cobras, also known as king cobras, are the world's longest venomous snakes. They are revered and feared in equal measure due to their impressive size, potent venom, and significant role in various cultures.

Habitat

Royal cobras are found predominantly in forests, bamboo thickets, mangrove swamps, and high-altitude grasslands across India, Southeast Asia, and Southern China. They prefer areas with dense undergrowth and access to water sources.

Behavior

Known for their intelligence and strong territorial instincts, royal cobras can be very aggressive when threatened. They are solitary creatures except during mating season and are known for their distinctive hood and intimidating display when provoked.

Interesting Facts

  • The king cobra's scientific name is Ophiophagus hannah, which means "snake-eater" as they primarily feed on other snakes.
  • They can reach lengths of up to 18 feet, making them the longest venomous snake in the world.
  • King cobras have a lifespan of around 20 years in the wild.
  • Despite their fearsome reputation, they tend to avoid humans and only attack when cornered or provoked.
